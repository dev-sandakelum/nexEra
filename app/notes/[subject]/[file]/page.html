Chapter 1

Fundamentals of Information Systems

Understanding the core building blocks of digital systems: how raw inputs become actionable insights.

Data

Raw facts or figures without any meaning. It is the basic input for processing (e.g., "105", "Red").

Information

Processed and organized data that has meaning and can be used for decision-making.

Knowledge

Understanding and awareness gained from information. It helps in solving problems.

Transforming Data into Information

Data is collected, processed, organized, and structured to add meaning. Valuable information must be accurate, timely, relevant, complete, and understandable.

Chapter 2

Systems & Classifications

A system is a set of interrelated components working together to achieve a specific goal.

Open System

Adaptive

Exchanges matter, energy, or information with its environment and adapts to external changes.

Example: Human Body

Closed System

Isolated

Isolated from its environment and does not exchange matter or energy freely.

Example: A Sealed Reaction

Computer-Based Information System (CBIS)

Hardware: Physical devices
Software: Control programs
People: Users and operators
Network: Connecting devices
Database: Organized facts
Procedures: Rules & methods
